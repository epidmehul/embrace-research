Our Projects

Three interconnected lines of research examining regional systems of emergency care across stroke, traumatic injury, and sepsis.

Stroke

Rural EMS Perspectives on Acute Stroke Care

Semi-structured interviews with rural EMS clinicians examining what is working — and what isn't — within current regional stroke systems.

Patient & Caregiver Experience with Acute Stroke

Qualitative interviews with stroke survivors and caregivers exploring their perspectives on the care they received across the full care continuum.

EMS Stroke Triage & Transport Model (ESTTM)

A computer simulation model estimating the impact of different EMS stroke triage and transport strategies on time-to-treatment and outcomes.

Traumatic Injury

Rural-Urban Differences in Prehospital Deaths Due to Injury in North Carolina

In EMS data, risks of prehospital death due to injury were 1.5 to 1.8 times higher in rural and suburban NC compared to urban areas.

Sepsis

Effectiveness of a Novel Rapid Infusion Device and Clinician Education for Early Fluid Therapy by EMS in Sepsis Patients

Combining EMS sepsis education with a hand-operated rapid infusion device (LifeFlow PLUS®) appears to significantly improve fluid delivery for sepsis patients.
